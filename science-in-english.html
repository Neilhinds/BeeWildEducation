Science in English

A project dedicated to supporting and expanding student understanding of science through the English language. Science in English is a long term project that allows students to immerse themselves in the world of science and develop their understanding of the world around them.

Content and Language Integrated Learning (CLIL)

Learning Science through English using a CLIL (Content and Language Integrated Learning) approach offers students a powerful and meaningful way to develop both subject knowledge and language skills at the same time. By studying scientific concepts in English, students are exposed to authentic vocabulary and real-world language used in global science, technology and research contexts. CLIL encourages active learning, critical thinking and problem-solving, as students explore, discuss and experiment while using English as a tool for communication rather than simply a subject to be studied. This integrated approach increases motivation, builds confidence in using English naturally, and helps learners develop transferable skills that support academic success, curiosity and a deeper understanding of the world around them.

Curiosity and Child-Led

Learning Science in English through a child- and curiosity-led approach places studentsâ€™ natural questions, interests and discoveries at the centre of the learning experience. Rather than following a fixed curriculum, lessons evolve from observation, exploration and experimentation, allowing children to investigate real-world phenomena in meaningful and engaging ways. English is used naturally as students describe what they see, ask questions, make predictions and share ideas, helping language develop alongside scientific thinking. This flexible approach nurtures curiosity, creativity and confidence, while encouraging a deeper understanding of scientific concepts and fostering a genuine love of learning driven by wonder and exploration.

Experiential Learning

Learning Science in English through experiential learning allows students to understand scientific concepts by actively engaging with them rather than simply learning about them. Through hands-on experiments, observation, exploration and real-life problem solving, students learn by doing, using English naturally to describe processes, ask questions and reflect on outcomes. This approach strengthens both scientific understanding and language acquisition, as vocabulary and structures are learned in meaningful, memorable contexts. Experiential learning increases motivation, builds confidence and supports deeper comprehension, helping students connect science to the real world while developing critical thinking and communication skills in English.

Outdoor Learning

Learning Science in English through outdoor education brings scientific concepts to life by placing students directly in the environments they are studying. Natural spaces become living classrooms where children can observe ecosystems, investigate materials, explore forces and notice change in real time. Using English in these authentic contexts helps students develop subject-specific vocabulary naturally, while encouraging discussion, collaboration and inquiry. Outdoor science learning enhances engagement, curiosity and wellbeing, supports deeper understanding through first-hand experience, and helps students make meaningful connections between scientific ideas, language and the world around them.

Why should you use BeeWild for Science in English?

Science in English is an investment in engaged, confident and future-ready learners. By combining language acquisition with curiosity-led, experiential and outdoor learning, this approach transforms science into an active, meaningful experience that students enjoy and remember. Schools benefit from higher motivation, improved communication skills and deeper understanding, while students gain the confidence to think critically, collaborate and use English naturally in real-world contexts. Welcoming Science in English means offering a modern, inclusive and inspiring educational experience that supports wellbeing, enriches the curriculum and prepares students for an increasingly international world.
